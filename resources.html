Presentation — September 2024

Alan Watts: Philosophy, Zen, and the Art of Being

"What if the harder you chase happiness, the further it slips away?"

Brief presentation on Alan Watts — the English philosopher who brought Eastern philosophy to the Western world. Covers his life and ideas on consciousness, the self, the nature of time, and the relationship between Zen Buddhism and Western thought. Explores the Backwards Law: the idea that pursuing something directly often pushes it further away.

Presentation — October 2024

Confucius (孔夫子): Ethics, Ritual, and the Moral Life

"If one cannot honor those closest to them, how can they be trusted to uphold responsibilities to others?"

An introduction to Confucius and the core ideals of Confucian philosophy. Covers Rén (仁) — benevolence and humanity; Lǐ (禮) — ritual and social harmony; Xiào (孝) — filial piety and respect for elders; and Yì (義) — righteousness and moral integrity. Presented by the Philosophy Club at the International School.

Presentation — June 2025

Moral Implications of Synthetic Intelligence

"If we breathe consciousness into artificial minds, what responsibilities come with that act? Are we gods, parents, engineers — or something else entirely?"

Explores artificial consciousness, personhood, and existential risk through the lens of philosophy. Covers the Turing Test, Roko's Basilisk, Searle's Chinese Room, Nagel's subjectivity problem, and the moral frameworks of Kant and Singer applied to synthetic beings. Presented at the International School STEM Fair.

Interview Presentation — November 2025

AI Ethics with Professor Jeff Behrends

"Are ethics even real — and if so, do they apply to machines the same way they apply to us?"

Slides from ESP's live interview with Professor Jeff Behrends, Director of Ethics at Harvard University. Covers the realism vs. anti-realism debate in ethics, the moral duty of "due consideration" in black-box AI, whether ethics for humans and AI are fundamentally different, and whether ethical constraints should be built in from the start or added later.
